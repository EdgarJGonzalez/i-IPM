Thoughts about refs:

* multiple maxima: Bolker 2008, Hilborn and Mangel (Ecological Detective), Etienne et al. (below), Gottesman et al. below (somewhat off-topic, but take a look at their figure S1!)
* complexity in models: Gelman and Hill 2006 Applied Regression Modeling have a nice discussion/picture; also see the Lavine et al. paper and associated papers (Lele et al. and others)
* identifiability: Lele et al on data cloning
* simulated annealing: very commonly used in seed dispersal inverse modeling (Ribbens et al 1994, Green et al 2004 below)

Etienne, Rampal S., Andrew M. Latimer, John A. Silander, and Richard M. Cowling. 2006. “Comment on ‘Neutral Ecological Theory Reveals Isolation and Rapid Speciation in a Biodiversity Hot Spot.’” Science 311 (5761): 610b. doi:10.1126/science.1121914.
Goffe, William L., Gary D. Ferrier, and John Rogers. 1994. “Global Optimization of Statistical Functions with Simulated Annealing.” Journal of Econometrics 60 (1–2): 65–99. doi:10.1016/0304-4076(94)90038-8.
Gottesman, William L., Andrew James Reagan, and Peter Sheridan Dodds. 2014. “Collective Philanthropy: Describing and Modeling the Ecology of Giving.” PLoS ONE 9 (7). doi:10.1371/journal.pone.0098876.
Greene, David F., Charles D. Canham, K. David Coates, and Philip T. Lepage. 2004. “An Evaluation of Alternative Dispersal Functions for Trees.” Journal of Ecology 92 (5): 758–66. doi:10.1111/j.0022-0477.2004.00921.x.
Lavine, Michael. 2010. “Living Dangerously with Big Fancy models.” Ecology 91 (12): 3487–3487. doi:10.1890/10-1124.1.
Lele, Subhash R., Khurram Nadeem, and Byron Schmuland. 2010. “Estimability and Likelihood Inference for Generalized Linear Mixed Models Using Data Cloning.” Journal of the American Statistical Association 105 (492): 1617–25. doi:10.1198/jasa.2010.tm09757.
Ribbens, Eric, John A. Silander, and Stephen W. Pacala. 1994. “Seedling Recruitment in Forests: Calibrating Models to Predict Patterns of Tree Seedling Dispersion.” Ecology 75 (6): 1794. doi:10.2307/1939638.
